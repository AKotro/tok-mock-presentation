To What Extent Should Artificial Intelligences Be Ethically Treated As Humans?

  • HAEF IB1 Theory Of Knowledge
  • Antony Kotronakis

Real Life Situation (RLS)

  • 2016 was a huge year for artificial intelligence (AI)

  • In January 2016, Google's AI "AlphaGo", created by DeepMind, defeated the world champion Lee Sedol

  • AlphaGo was able to master the game by learning from millions of samples of real games

  • Go is said to be the "most complex professional game man has ever devised"

  • Many experts believe that it relies heavily on human intuition

  • For this reason, this was a very big accomplishment for the world of AIs

  • From this RLS we can extract the knowledge question: "To What Extent Can Artificial Intelligences Be Ethically Treated As Humans?"

Knowledge Claim

  • Ethics, as an area of knowledge, deals with the morality of human thoughts and actions

  • This knowledge question, thus poses an ethical dilemma

  • Historically, many of humans' morally "wrong" actions come from their fear of the unknown

  • This is the case for many people when it comes to artificial Intelligences

  • Therefore one knowledge claim that can be made is: "Artificial intelligences should never be treated as humans."

Knowledge Claim

  • Many highly esteemed scientists, such as Stephen Hawking have expressed their fear of possible dominion over humanity by AIs

  • Due to this reason, many believe that AIs should never become socially equal to humans

  • Indeed, there are many ethical implications when it comes to the applied ethics of dealing with the rights of AIs

  • Take voting rights as an example: to decide if an AI's vote is equal to that of a human, we have to examine a sub-knowledge question: "What makes humans different than other forms of life?"

  • Technically, a human is a life form that can us the ways of knowing, such as sense perception, intuition and reason

  • This allows humans to make ethical decisions and be "smarter in general"

Counter Claim

  • However, a counter claim "Artificial intelligences should be treated as humans" can be developed

  • One can argue that already, as seen in the real life situation used, artificial intelligences have started to develop certain human qualities

  • From the RLS, AlphaGo was able to use intuition, to come back from significant mistake that it had made in the last game against the world champion

  • With the rate of growth in the industry of artificial intelligence development, it is very likely that AIs will reach a point where they will be able to display almost every human quality

  • Then, the only thing left to distinguish between humans and AIs would be biological and so there is no reason for AIs not to be treated equally

Other Real Life Situations

  • In March 2017, Google's DeepMind, the same company that worked on AlphaGo developed a program that was able to play 10 classic Atari games on the same skill level as a human on its own

  • In October 2016, Microsoft's speech recognition AI was able to be on par with a human's performance in a conversational speech task

Conclusion

  • As is evident, Google's AlphaGo has sparked a large ethical dilemma as to the moral implications that arise with the development of artificial intelligences

  • These advances in artificial intelligence show that in the end, AIs will most likley be able to develop and use the same ways of knowing as humans

  • In that case, the first knowledge claim seems to become the right answer, which states that AIs should be treated the same as humans

  • However, one cannot predict the future and it is possible that this may never happen and that the second knowledge claim, that AIs should never be treated the same as humans would apply
